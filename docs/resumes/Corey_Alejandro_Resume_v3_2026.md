## Executive Summary

I design safety-critical systems that prevent harm across AI, human decision-making, and learning environments — not as a feature bolted on at the end, but as the architecture itself.

My work spans four safety domains: **Epistemic Safety** (preventing false claims and hallucinated outputs), **Human Safety** (preventing systems from dis-enabling people), **Cognitive Safety** (preventing misunderstanding and flawed mental models), and **Empirical Safety** (proving safety through measurement and verifiable evidence chains). SentinelOS is the operating layer — six invariants enforced at every system boundary.

Before AI, I spent 35 years designing safe learning environments in education, instructional design, and student affairs. The doctrine was the same: every failure is a signal about the system, not the person inside it. I am not an AI engineer who added safety. I am a safety systems designer who arrived at AI.
